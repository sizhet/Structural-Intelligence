# Structural Intelligence

## Foundations, Frontiers, and Future Directions

### A Unified Research Program for Systems, Decisions, Trajectories, and Collective Intelligence

---

## Overview

Structural Intelligence (SI) is a research program that investigates intelligence from a structural perspective.

Rather than focusing primarily on prediction, generation, or optimization, Structural Intelligence studies how intelligent systems:

* understand structures,
* make decisions within structures,
* evolve along feasible trajectories,
* participate in collective intelligence systems.

The framework is organized around a common foundation layer and three major frontier directions.

## Three Frontier Directions

---

![./docs/figures/Fig-002-SI-Frontier-Triangle.png](./docs/figures/Fig-002-SI-Frontier-Triangle.png)

---

### CGCCC

System Structural Intelligence.

Core Question:

> What exists?

Focus:

* system structures
* calling graphs
* task-action networks
* human-AI ecosystems
* future networked intelligence

---

### PDS / ASI

Decision Structural Intelligence.

Core Question:

> What should be done?

Focus:

* policies
* decision systems
* governance
* autonomous intelligence
* structural control planes

---

### FTI

Trajectory Structural Intelligence.

Core Question:

> How can change occur safely?

Focus:

* trajectories
* evolution
* function tunnels
* preservation
* feasible transformations

---

## Research Vision

Structural Intelligence seeks to complement existing AI paradigms by providing explicit mechanisms for:

* structural understanding,
* policy-controlled decision making,
* trajectory-preserving evolution,
* collective intelligence systems.

The long-term objective is not merely to build systems that generate outputs, but systems that understand structures, govern actions, preserve functionality, and participate in large-scale human-AI intelligence ecosystems.
